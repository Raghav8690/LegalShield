### 🔒 Your Rights: Illegal Eviction (Maharashtra, India)

**Jurisdiction:** Maharashtra, India
**Legal Area:** Housing & Tenant Rights

#### What the Law Says

Under the **Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999** and the **Transfer of Property Act, 1882**,
a landlord **cannot** evict a tenant through self-help measures like changing locks, cutting
utilities, or physical intimidation.

The law requires your landlord to:
1. Serve a **formal written notice** (typically 30 days for month-to-month tenancies)
2. File an **eviction suit in the Rent Court** if you don't vacate after notice
3. Obtain a **court order** before any eviction can take place

A 3-day verbal threat to change locks is **not a valid eviction process**.

#### What Your Landlord CANNOT Do
❌ Change the locks while you're living there
❌ Cut off water, electricity, or other utilities
❌ Remove your belongings from the property
❌ Physically intimidate or threaten you
❌ Enter your premises without your permission

All of these are considered **illegal self-help eviction** and may constitute criminal
intimidation under the **Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023, Section 351**.

#### Key Deadlines
⏰ Your landlord must provide minimum **30 days written notice** (for monthly tenants)
⏰ Even after notice, they must go through court — which takes months
