### 💼 Your Rights: Unpaid Wages (Texas, US)

**Jurisdiction:** Texas, United States
**Legal Area:** Employment & Wage Rights

#### What the Law Says

Under the **Texas Payday Law (Texas Labor Code, Chapter 61)**, your employer is legally
required to pay you on your regularly scheduled payday. "Payroll issues" is not a legally
valid excuse to withhold your wages.

Key protections:
- Employer **must pay all wages earned** on the designated payday
- If employment ends, final wages are due within **6 days** (if fired) or next regular
  payday (if you quit)
- The **Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)** also requires timely payment of at least minimum
  wage for all hours worked
- **Retaliation is illegal** — your employer cannot fire or punish you for filing a wage claim

#### What Your Employer CANNOT Do
❌ Withhold wages without a valid, specific legal reason
❌ Delay payment indefinitely due to "payroll issues"
❌ Retaliate against you for asking about your pay or filing a complaint
❌ Pay less than minimum wage ($7.25/hr federal, check local ordinances)

### Your Next 3 Actions

1. **Immediately:** Document everything —
   - Save any texts, emails, or messages where your employer acknowledges the unpaid wages
   - Note the exact dates of your last payment and all unpaid work days
   - Keep your pay stubs, timesheets, or time clock records

2. **This week:** I can draft a formal wage demand letter to your employer. This creates
   a paper trail and often prompts payment. Would you like me to draft that?

3. **If they still don't pay (within 14 days):** File a **Wage Claim** with the
   **Texas Workforce Commission (TWC)**:
   - 🌐 Online: [twc.texas.gov/jobseekers/how-submit-wage-claim](https://twc.texas.gov)
   - 📞 Phone: **800-832-9243**
   - ⏰ **Deadline: 180 days from the date wages were originally due**
