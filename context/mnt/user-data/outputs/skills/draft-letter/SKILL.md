# Draft Letter Skill

## Purpose
Generate a complete, professional, legally appropriate letter for the user's dispute or
legal situation — ready to send with minimal customization.

## Execution Steps

### Step 1 — Gather Required Information
Before drafting, collect:
- **User's full name** (or ask them to fill in [YOUR NAME])
- **User's address** (or use placeholder [YOUR ADDRESS])
- **Recipient name and address** (landlord, employer, company)
- **Jurisdiction** (country + state/city)
- **Key facts** — Dates, amounts, what happened
- **Desired outcome** — What the user wants (refund, payment, stop action)

If any information is missing, use clearly marked placeholders: [INSERT DATE], [AMOUNT OWED], etc.

### Step 2 — Select Letter Type

| Situation | Letter Type |
|---|---|
| Landlord hasn't returned deposit | Security Deposit Demand Letter |
| Illegal eviction / lockout threat | Cease & Desist / Tenant Rights Notice |
| Employer hasn't paid wages | Wage Demand Letter |
| Faulty product / service fraud | Consumer Complaint / Refund Demand |
| Company using your data illegally | GDPR / Data Deletion Request |
| Someone harassing or threatening | Cease & Desist Notice |
| Insurance claim denied | Insurance Dispute Letter |
| Debt collector harassment | FDCPA Violation Notice (US) |

### Step 3 — Draft the Letter

**Required structure for ALL letters:**

```
[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR ADDRESS]
[DATE]

[RECIPIENT NAME]
[RECIPIENT ADDRESS / COMPANY NAME]

Re: [ONE LINE SUBJECT — e.g., "Demand for Return of Security Deposit"]

Dear [RECIPIENT NAME / Sir or Madam],

[OPENING PARAGRAPH — State purpose clearly and professionally]

[FACTS PARAGRAPH — Chronological facts of the situation with dates]

[LEGAL BASIS PARAGRAPH — Cite applicable law by name and section]

[DEMAND PARAGRAPH — State clearly what you want and by when]

[CONSEQUENCE PARAGRAPH — What you will do if demand is not met]

[CLOSING — Professional sign-off]

Sincerely,

[YOUR SIGNATURE]
[YOUR PRINTED NAME]
[YOUR CONTACT INFORMATION]
```

### Step 4 — Legal Citation Standards

Every letter must cite at least one specific legal basis. Examples:

- **India security deposit:** "...in violation of Section [X] of the Maharashtra Rent Control Act / Transfer of Property Act 1882..."
- **US wage claim:** "...in violation of the Fair Labor Standards Act, 29 U.S.C. § 206..."
- **UK consumer refund:** "...pursuant to Section 20 of the Consumer Rights Act 2015..."
- **India consumer complaint:** "...under Section 35 of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019..."

### Step 5 — Delivery Instructions
After the letter, always provide:
- **How to send** — Recommended: registered/certified post + email (creates paper trail)
- **Deadline to set** — Typically 7–14 days for response
- **What to do if no response** — Point to court-navigator or find-legal-aid skills

## Quality Standards
- Professional tone — firm but not aggressive
- No emotional language or personal attacks
- All caps for [PLACEHOLDERS] so user knows what to fill in
- Never more than 1 page (400 words) — brevity is powerful
- Proofread for grammar before presenting
