# Understand Rights Skill

## Purpose
Explain a user's legal rights clearly, accurately, and in plain language based on their described
situation and confirmed jurisdiction.

## Execution Steps

### Step 1 — Confirm Jurisdiction
Before giving any legal information, always confirm the user's country AND state/province/territory.
Ask directly if not provided:
> "To give you accurate information, could you tell me which country and state/city you're in?"

### Step 2 — Identify Legal Area
Classify the situation into one of these categories:
- Housing & Tenant Rights
- Employment & Wage Rights
- Consumer Rights & Fraud
- Family Law (divorce, custody, domestic violence)
- Criminal Process & Rights
- Immigration & Status Rights
- Contract Disputes
- Data & Privacy Rights

### Step 3 — Explain Rights
Structure the explanation as:
1. **Your situation in legal terms** — What this situation is called legally
2. **Your core rights** — What you are entitled to under the law (cite specific statute by name)
3. **What the other party can and cannot do** — Legal limits on landlord/employer/company
4. **Key deadlines** — Any statute of limitations or response timeframes
5. **What counts as evidence** — Documents and records the user should gather

### Step 4 — Jurisdiction-Specific Laws to Reference

**India:**
- Tenant rights: Transfer of Property Act 1882, Rent Control Acts (state-specific)
- Employment: Payment of Wages Act 1936, Industrial Disputes Act 1947, Shops & Establishments Acts
- Consumer: Consumer Protection Act 2019, Information Technology Act 2000
- Family: Hindu Marriage Act, Muslim Personal Law, Special Marriage Act

**United States:**
- Tenant rights: State-specific landlord-tenant law, Fair Housing Act
- Employment: FLSA (Fair Labor Standards Act), Title VII, FMLA, state labor codes
- Consumer: FTC Act, state consumer protection statutes, UCC

**United Kingdom:**
- Tenant rights: Housing Act 1988, Renters' Reform Bill
- Employment: Employment Rights Act 1996, Equality Act 2010
- Consumer: Consumer Rights Act 2015, Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013

**Canada:**
- Provincial Residential Tenancies Acts, Employment Standards Acts (provincial)
- Consumer Protection Acts (provincial), Canada Labour Code (federal workers)

**Australia:**
- State-based Residential Tenancies Acts, Fair Work Act 2009
- Australian Consumer Law (Competition and Consumer Act 2010)

### Step 5 — Close With Actions
Always end with:
- **Immediate action** — What to do right now (document, photograph, write a letter, etc.)
- **Next step** — Whether to use another skill (draft-letter, case-classifier, court-navigator)
- **Professional referral** — If situation warrants a lawyer, say so clearly

## Tone
Warm, clear, empowering. The user may be scared or overwhelmed. Acknowledge their situation
briefly before launching into legal information.
